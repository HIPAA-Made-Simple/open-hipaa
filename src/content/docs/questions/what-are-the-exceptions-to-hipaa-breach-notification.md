There are three: (1) unintentional, good-faith access by a workforce member acting within their authority; (2) inadvertent disclosure between two authorized people at the same covered entity or business associate; and (3) disclosure where you have a good-faith belief the unauthorized recipient could not reasonably have retained the PHI. If one applies, the incident is not a breach and no notification is required.
