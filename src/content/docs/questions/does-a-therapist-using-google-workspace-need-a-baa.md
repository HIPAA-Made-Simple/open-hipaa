Yes. If any protected health information (PHI) touches your Google Workspace
account — client emails, session notes in Drive, appointment details in
Calendar — HIPAA requires a signed Business Associate Agreement (BAA) with
Google before that use is compliant.

## The short version

- Google offers a BAA at no extra cost on **Business Starter and above**.
- You accept it in the Admin Console (Account settings → Legal and compliance).
- A **personal @gmail.com account can never be HIPAA-compliant** — Google does
  not offer a BAA for consumer accounts.

## What the BAA does and doesn't cover

The BAA covers the core Workspace services (Gmail, Drive, Calendar, Meet, and
others Google lists as "included functionality"). It does not make every
Google product compliant, and it does not configure anything for you — you
still need to turn on 2-step verification, restrict sharing defaults, and
document the setup in your risk analysis.
